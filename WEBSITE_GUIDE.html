1 · Start here
👋
What this is, in one minute
This is your complete business website for Fintex Global Group — public pages your clients see, plus a private practice portal where you run your team, clients, jobs and onboarding. Everything is built as plain files (HTML/CSS/JavaScript), so it is fast, free to host, and you fully own it. You do not need to know any code to use it. This guide walks you from the files on your computer all the way to a live website.
📦 You received a folder of files. The whole website is inside it. Nothing is hidden on someone else's server — it is all yours.
🖥️
Preview it on your own computer first
Before putting it online, look at it locally:
  1. Unzip the folder you received.
  2. Double-click index.html — your home page opens in your web browser.
  3. Click around. Every link, the menu, the contact form and the portal all work offline.
When you are happy, you publish it (Section 6).

The sections, explained
  • Dashboard — your key numbers at a glance, a 7-day website-visits chart, and recent activity.
  • Job Manager — a drag-and-drop board (To Do → In Progress → Review → Done). Click a card to edit; drag to move. Inspired by Monday.com.
  • Leads — every enquiry from your contact form lands here automatically. Convert a lead into a client with one click.
  • Onboarding — start an ATO/TPB-aligned intake for a new client, track the checklist (ID, TFN/ABN, engagement letter, AML, ATO agent linking), and copy a personal intake link to send them. Inspired by Ignition + FYI.
  • Sign-up Approvals — new clients who sign up on the website land here. Review their details and click Approve to create their login automatically (they set their own password on first sign-in). A bell (top-right) and a red badge show how many are waiting.
  • Reports — generate management reports and export each as PDF, CSV or Excel (.xlsx). Sign-ups and contact enquiries both feed the Leads report — your list for follow-up and advertising/remarketing.
  • Content & Banners — edit the homepage banner slides and client testimonials yourself, no code needed. Add, edit or remove them and they update on the site.
  • Onboarding Form — build and edit the client intake form like Google Forms or MS Forms: add/edit/reorder/remove questions, set field types and which are required. No code, no HTML.
  • Client Database — all your clients, searchable; add, edit, put on hold, or delete.
  • Documents — a Hubdoc-style inbox of everything clients submit through their portal.
  • Team & Access — staff and roles (above).
  • Settings — your company details, sign-up & notification options, plus where you paste integration keys (Section 5).
  • Audit Log — a timestamped record of key actions, for accountability and compliance.
🆕
Client sign-ups & approving them
Prospective clients can now create their own account. On portal.html (Client tab) there is a “Create your client account” link. It opens the same friendly form as the website Contact page (name, email, phone, region, service, message).
  1. The client fills it in and submits.
  2. It appears under Sign-up Approvals in your portal, the bell (top-right) shows a red dot, and — if you have set up EmailJS — your team gets an email alert. The request is also copied into your Leads report.
  3. You review the details and click Approve & create login. The portal instantly creates their client account and starts their onboarding.
  4. You are shown a one-time password to share securely. The client is forced to set their own password the first time they log in.
The account stays active while you work on their jobs; you can put it on hold any time from the Client Database.
⚙️ In Settings → Client sign-up & notifications you choose a standard default password or a random one each time, and set the team email + WhatsApp number used for alerts.
🔔
Notifications (the bell)
When you are signed in as a team member, a bell appears at the top-right. It collects new sign-ups and new website enquiries. The red number is how many are unread. Click it to read them, jump straight to a sign-up to review, or mark them all read.
Like all portal data, the bell and approvals live in the browser on your device by default (see Section 4). For your whole team to share the same live notifications and approvals from any device — and for sign-up alerts to be sent automatically server-side — connect a backend and EmailJS (Sections 4 & 5, and the Integration Guide).
✨
Editing banners & testimonials (no code)
Go to Content & Banners in the portal. You can add, edit or delete:
  • Homepage banner slides — the rotating promo banners at the top of the home page. Set a region/colour, headline, sub-text and up to two buttons.
  • Client testimonials — the review cards. Set the region, star rating, quote, name and role.
Save, then open index.html — your changes show immediately. This replaces having to hand-edit HTML every time.
📝
Building the onboarding form (like Google/MS Forms)
Go to Onboarding Form in the portal. This is the intake form your clients fill in from their portal. You can add a field (short text, long text, email, phone, number, date, dropdown, checkbox/consent, or an info note), edit any question, mark it required, add help text or dropdown options, reorder with the ↑ ↓ buttons, delete a field, or Restore default to bring back the full recommended set. Whatever you set is exactly what clients see — no code.
🛡️ Compliance — important: the form does not collect a client's Tax File Number (TFN) or ABN, and you should never add fields that do. Under the ATO and the Privacy (Tax File Number) Rule 2015, a TFN must not be collected through an ordinary web form. Tax identifiers are verified separately through secure ATO/TPB channels (ATO Online services and client–agent linking). The default form includes a security notice and consent that reflect this.
🌐
Publishing content live (so every visitor sees your edits)
Because edits are saved in your browser (Section 4), banner/testimonial changes you make in the portal show on your device immediately, and on the live site for visitors using that same browser. To publish content changes to all visitors, you have two clean options:
  1. Simple: make the change, then ask whoever maintains the site to copy the updated text into index.html and re-publish (drag the folder back onto Netlify — Section 6). Good for occasional changes.
  2. Automatic: connect a free backend (Supabase/Firebase, Section 4). Then the homepage reads banners and testimonials from the cloud, so every visitor sees your portal edits instantly with no re-publishing.
The homepage is already wired to use your portal-managed banners and testimonials when they are available — so the automatic option is a small, contained change.

4 · Where your data lives (important & honest)
💾
Right now: your browser
Out of the box, the portal saves everything in your web browser's private storage (called localStorage). This is genuinely useful: it works instantly, costs nothing, needs no server, and is perfect for a live demo or a single person running the practice on one computer.
⚠️ The honest limitation: browser storage is per-device and per-browser. Data you enter on your office laptop will not appear on your phone or on a colleague's computer, and clearing your browser data erases it. The same applies to website footfall — it counts visits on each visitor's own device, not a shared total.
This is normal for a static website and totally fine to launch with. When you want true multi-user, multi-device shared data, you connect a free backend — next.
☁️
When you're ready: a free backend (multi-user, real sync)
To let your whole team share the same live data from any device, connect a free "backend as a service". The two easiest are Supabase and Firebase — both have generous free tiers that comfortably cover a small firm.

In plain terms, a developer (a few hours' work) swaps the browser-storage calls in portal.js for calls to Supabase/Firebase. Everything you see stays the same; the data simply lives in the cloud instead of one browser. The portal was written with this upgrade in mind — all data goes through one small helper (DB), so it is a clean, contained change.
💡 You can launch today on browser storage, show it to clients, and upgrade later without rebuilding the site.
📈
Real website footfall (cross-visitor)
For accurate visitor numbers across everyone (not just your own device), add a free, privacy-friendly analytics tool and paste its snippet into your pages. Two great free options:
  • Cloudflare Web Analytics — free, no cookie banner needed, very private.
  • Google Analytics 4 (GA4) — free, the industry standard, more detailed.
Full step-by-step in the Integration Guide. Record the provider and ID in Settings → Analytics so your team knows what is connected.

7 · Keeping it healthy
🧰
Simple maintenance routine
  • Weekly: skim Leads and reply quickly; move jobs along the board.
  • Monthly: export a Leads + Jobs report (PDF/Excel) for your records; add a blog post for SEO.
  • Quarterly: review team roles and remove anyone who has left; check your contact details are current.

8 · Compliance notes (Australia)
⚖️
ATO, TPB & Privacy — what the site already supports
Your onboarding flow is built around Australian expectations for registered tax/BAS agents:
  • Tax Practitioners Board (TPB) — client identity verification (proof-of-identity), a signed engagement letter, and the Code of Professional Conduct are represented in the onboarding checklist.
  • ATO — TFN/ABN capture and the client-agent linking/authorisation step are included.
  • AML/CTF — a fit-and-proper / AML check item is included where applicable.
  • Privacy Act 1988 (APPs) — collect only what you need, tell clients why, and store it securely (a key reason to move to a proper backend with access controls before holding sensitive data at scale).
This website helps you follow good practice, but it is software, not legal advice. Confirm your obligations with the TPB, ATO and a qualified adviser, and make sure your privacy/consent wording matches how you actually handle data.
