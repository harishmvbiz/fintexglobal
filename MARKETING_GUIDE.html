📣 Low & No-Cost Marketing Guide
How to attract clients in Australia (primary), UAE and India — without an ad budget. Ordered by impact for an accounting & tax firm.

On this page

AustraliaUAEIndiaYour edge30-day plan
You don't need an ad budget to win accounting & tax clients — trust and visibility matter more. This guide is ordered by impact for a professional services firm. Australia is the priority, with UAE and India sections after. Most of this is free; a few items are optional low-cost.
⚖️ Advertising rule for AU tax practitioners: the TPB Code of Professional Conduct requires that anything you publish is not false, misleading or deceptive. Don't guarantee refunds or specific outcomes. Keep claims truthful and evidence-based.
Australia (primary focus)
⭐
1. Google Business Profile — do this first
Free, and the single highest-impact action for a local firm. It puts you on Google Maps and in the local results when someone searches "accountant near me" or "tax agent Brisbane".
  1. Create it at business.google.com and verify your Brisbane QLD address.
  2. Fill everything: services, hours, photos, your website link.
  3. Ask every happy client for a Google review — reviews are the #1 driver of local ranking and trust. Send them the review link by email or WhatsApp.
Free · highest ROI
🔎
2. Local SEO on your own site
Your site is already SEO-friendly (clean pages, a sitemap). Strengthen it for free:
  • Mention your locations naturally in headings and text (Brisbane, Queensland, Australia-wide).
  • Give each service its own clear section so it can rank for that search.
  • Submit your sitemap.xml in free Google Search Console so Google indexes you faster.
Free
✍️
3. Content / blog — compounding free traffic
Your blog.html exists for this. Write short, genuinely useful posts answering what clients actually Google:
  • "BAS due dates 2025–26 for small business"
  • "Sole trader vs company: tax differences in Australia"
  • "What records the ATO expects you to keep"
  • "Crypto & tax in Australia — the basics"
One quality post a month quietly brings in searchers for years. Reuse each post as a LinkedIn update.
Free · compounding
💼
4. LinkedIn — where business clients are
  • Post 2–4×/month: a tax tip, a deadline reminder, a short client win (no names/figures without consent).
  • Optimise the founder's profile (CA · CPA Australia · ACCA) — credentials build instant trust.
  • Engage in Australian small-business and startup groups; answer questions helpfully, don't hard-sell.
Free
📂
5. Free business directories
List your firm (free tiers) where Australians search for accountants — consistency of your name, address & phone across these also helps Google:
  • Yellow Pages & True Local & Hotfrog & Yelp AU
  • CPA Australia's "Find a CPA" / public practice directory (you're a member — use it)
  • Local Chamber of Commerce & your suburb's business directory
Free
🤝
6. Referrals & partnerships (your best channel)
Accounting grows on word-of-mouth.
  • Ask satisfied clients directly for referrals — a simple, polite ask works.
  • Partner with complementary professionals: bookkeepers, mortgage brokers, financial planners, migration agents, lawyers. Refer to each other.
  • Offer a small thank-you for referrals where allowed.
Free
👥
7. Communities & local presence
  • Be helpful in Australian small-business Facebook groups and forums like Whirlpool finance threads (answer, don't spam).
  • Offer a free 20-minute "tax health check" webinar or local workshop for small-business owners or migrants.
  • Target the Indian-Australian community (a natural fit given the founder's background) via community associations and events.
Free
📧
8. Email & your existing pipeline
Every lead in your portal is a relationship. Send a light monthly email: a deadline reminder, one tip, one offer. Free tools: Mailchimp or Brevo free tiers. Re-engaging past enquiries is cheaper than finding new ones.
Free tier
💲
9. Optional paid (small budget, high intent)
If you later want to spend a little: Google Search Ads on high-intent terms ("tax agent [suburb]", "BAS help") let you set a tiny daily cap (e.g. A$5–10/day) and only pay per click. Start small, track which keywords convert, and pause the rest.
Keep ad copy truthful per TPB rules. Track results before scaling.
UAE
🏙️
Dubai-focused, low cost
  • Google Business Profile for the Business Bay office — verify and gather reviews.
  • LinkedIn is very strong in the UAE for B2B — post on corporate tax, VAT/FTA compliance, free-zone setup.
  • Network in free-zone communities and business councils (e.g. Indian Business & Professional Council, chambers of commerce).
  • List on UAE directories like Yellow Pages UAE and Connect.ae.
  • WhatsApp is the default business channel — your site's WhatsApp button is already set for UAE.
Mostly free
India
High-volume, free channels
  • Google Business Profile for the India entity + reviews.
  • JustDial and IndiaMART free listings — heavily used to find CAs and tax services.
  • LinkedIn + WhatsApp Business for client communication and referrals.
  • Content on GST, ITR filing, and India–Australia/UAE cross-border matters — your multi-jurisdiction edge is rare and very shareable.
  • Local CA community groups and referral networks.
Mostly free
Your unfair advantage
Lead with "Beyond Borders"
Very few firms credibly serve Australia + UAE + India under one roof with a founder holding CA (India), CPA Australia and ACCA (UK). Make cross-border the hero of your marketing: expats, migrants, NRIs, and businesses operating across these three markets are an underserved, high-value niche. Every post, page and pitch should reinforce that you solve the multi-country problem others can't.
A simple 30-day starter plan
✅
Do these in order
  1. Week 1: Set up Google Business Profile (AU) + request your first 5 reviews. Submit sitemap to Search Console.
  2. Week 2: Optimise the founder's LinkedIn; publish one blog post; share it on LinkedIn.
  3. Week 3: Add free directory listings (AU, UAE, India). Reach out to 3 potential referral partners.
  4. Week 4: Send your first monthly email to existing leads; plan next month's blog topic. Review which channels brought enquiries (check Leads + analytics).
📈 Measure what works using your portal's Leads report + your analytics tool, then double down on the channels that actually bring clients.
