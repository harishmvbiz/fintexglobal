🔌 Integration Guide
Connect your site to free services for email alerts, spreadsheets, real analytics, and a shared cloud database. All optional, all free to start.

Your website works fully on its own. These integrations are optional upgrades that connect it to free outside services so you get email alerts, spreadsheets and real visitor stats. Do them in any order, whenever you like. Each is free on the tiers below.
1 · Formspree — receive form emails (easiest)
✉️
Get contact-form submissions by email
Formspree turns your existing contact form into emails — no coding.
  1. Sign up free at formspree.io.
  2. Create a new form; it gives you an endpoint like https://formspree.io/f/abcdwxyz.
  3. Open contact.html, find the comment that starts // TODO: Replace with real form endpoint, and add this just below it:
fetch('https://formspree.io/f/YOUR_ID',{ method:'POST', headers:{'Content-Type':'application/json'}, body:JSON.stringify(data) });
Save, publish, and submit a test. You'll get an email.
Free: 50 submissions / month
2 · EmailJS — instant lead alerts
🔔
Be notified the moment a lead arrives
  1. Sign up at emailjs.com.
  2. Add an email service (connect Gmail/Outlook) → note the Service ID.
  3. Create an email template using variables like {{name}}, {{email}}, {{message}} → note the Template ID.
  4. Copy your Public Key from Account.
  5. Paste all three into the portal under Settings → EmailJS (so your team has a record), and add the EmailJS snippet to contact.html per the EmailJS docs.
<script src="https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/npm/@emailjs/browser/dist/email.min.js"></script> <script>emailjs.init("YOUR_PUBLIC_KEY");</script> // then in submitForm, after saving the lead: emailjs.send("SERVICE_ID","TEMPLATE_ID",data);
Free: 200 emails / month
3 · Google Sheets — mirror every lead
📊
Push leads into a spreadsheet automatically
  1. Create a Google Sheet with headers: timestamp, name, email, region, service, message.
  2. Extensions → Apps Script, paste the code below, and Deploy → New deployment → Web app (Execute as: Me; Access: Anyone). Copy the Web App URL.
  3. Paste that URL into the portal under Settings → Google Sheets sync, and into contact.html as a fetch (same pattern as Formspree).
function doPost(e){ var s=SpreadsheetApp.getActiveSheet(); var d=JSON.parse(e.postData.contents); s.appendRow([d.timestamp,d.name,d.email,d.region,d.service,d.message]); return ContentService.createTextOutput("ok"); }
Free with any Google account
4 · Website analytics — real footfall
📈
Option A — Cloudflare Web Analytics (private, no banner)
  1. Sign up at cloudflare.com/web-analytics.
  2. Add your site; copy the small <script> beacon it gives you.
  3. Paste it just before </body> on every page (or only the ones you care about).
  4. Record "Cloudflare" in Settings → Analytics.
Privacy-friendly and cookie-free — usually no cookie-consent banner required. Great default for a professional services site.
📉
Option B — Google Analytics 4 (detailed)
  1. Create a free property at analytics.google.com → get a Measurement ID like G-XXXXXXX.
  2. Paste the GA4 gtag snippet before </body> on every page.
  3. Record "GA4" and the ID in Settings → Analytics.
GA4 uses cookies — add a short privacy/cookie note to your site to stay compliant.
5 · Backend (multi-user shared data)
☁️
Supabase or Firebase — when you outgrow browser storage
To share live portal data across your whole team and every device, connect a free backend. The portal funnels all reads/writes through one helper object (DB in portal.js), so a developer points that at Supabase or Firebase without changing the screens.
  • Supabase — free Postgres database + auth + row-level security. Closest to a traditional database; excellent for client records.
  • Firebase — free Firestore + auth; very quick to wire up.
Either gives you proper logins, permissions and secure storage for sensitive client data (important under the Privacy Act). Budget a few hours of developer time.
Both have free tiers suited to a small firm
6 · Sign-up alerts — WhatsApp & email
🔔
How a new sign-up notifies your team
When someone signs up on the website, three things happen straight away: it appears under Sign-up Approvals in the portal, the bell shows a red dot, and the request is copied into your Leads report. To also push that alert out to email and WhatsApp:
  • Email (automatic): set up EmailJS (Section 2) and save your Service ID, Template ID and Public Key in Settings → EmailJS. Then enter your team email in Settings → Client sign-up & notifications. New sign-ups will email your team automatically — no server needed.
  • WhatsApp (one-tap, no backend): enter your team WhatsApp number (digits only, e.g. 61412345678) in Settings → Client sign-up & notifications. After submitting, the client sees a “Message us on WhatsApp” button pre-filled with their details that opens a chat to your number.
  • WhatsApp (fully automatic): truly hands-off WhatsApp sending needs a paid gateway such as the WhatsApp Business Cloud API (Meta) or Twilio for WhatsApp. A developer connects it to your backend (Section 5) so each sign-up fires a message with no clicks.
Set the default/random starting password and team contact details in Settings → Client sign-up & notifications. Approved clients are always forced to set their own password on first login.
7 · Advertising & the Leads report
📣
Turn enquiries into low-cost marketing
Every contact-form enquiry and every website sign-up flows into Leads in the portal (sign-ups are tagged with source signup). Use it as your marketing list:
  1. Open Leads (or Reports → Leads) and export to CSV or Excel.
  2. Use the list for follow-up emails, a monthly newsletter, or to build remarketing/Custom Audiences in Google Ads or Meta Ads (upload emails as a customer list).
  3. Review which region and service generate the most enquiries to decide where to focus spend.
⚖️ Marketing compliance: only contact people who agreed to hear from you (the sign-up and contact forms include a consent tick), always include an unsubscribe option (Australian Spam Act 2003), and follow each ad platform's customer-list policies. Keep claims truthful per TPB advertising rules. See the Marketing Guide for the full low/no-cost plan across Australia, UAE and India.
8 · Secure identity & tax-ID handling (TFN / ABN)
🛡️
Never collect a TFN or ABN through the website
The client onboarding form deliberately does not ask for a Tax File Number (TFN) or ABN, and you should never add a field that does. Under the ATO and the Privacy (Tax File Number) Rule 2015, a TFN must not be collected via an ordinary web form, and the Tax Practitioners Board (TPB) proof-of-identity process is handled through secure channels — not a public website.
  1. Identify clients using the TPB-recommended process (e.g. 100-point ID check) in person, by video, or through a secure document portal — recorded on the Onboarding checklist in the portal.
  2. Link the client to your practice via ATO Online services for agents (client–agent linking) — this is where tax identifiers are confirmed, inside the ATO's secure environment.
  3. If you must receive a document containing a TFN, use encrypted/secure file transfer (e.g. your backend in Section 5 with access controls), never plain email or a web form.
The onboarding form (managed in Portal → Onboarding Form) shows clients a short security notice and a consent field reflecting this. Keep both when you customise the form.
⚖️ This is general implementation guidance, not legal advice. Confirm your obligations with the ATO, TPB and the OAIC (Privacy Act 1988) for your specific practice.
